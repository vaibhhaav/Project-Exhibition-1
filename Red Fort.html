Red Fort

The **Red Fort**, or **Lal Qila**, is one of India's most iconic monuments and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Located in Delhi, it was built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in 1638 when he shifted his capital from Agra to Delhi. Constructed with red sandstone, the fort stands as a magnificent example of Mughal architecture combined with Persian, Timurid, and Indian styles.

The fort’s massive red sandstone walls stretch over 2 kilometers and enclose a complex of palaces, gardens, and mosques. The main entrance, the **Lahori Gate**, is a focal point during India's Independence Day celebrations. Inside, the **Diwan-i-Aam** (Hall of Public Audience) and **Diwan-i-Khas** (Hall of Private Audience) showcase intricate carvings and luxurious decor, symbolizing the opulence of the Mughal era.

The **Naubat Khana**, or Drum House, once announced the arrival of dignitaries, while the **Moti Masjid** (Pearl Mosque) and **Rang Mahal** (Palace of Colors) add to the fort's grandeur. The gardens, inspired by Persian Charbagh layouts, enhance its elegance.

Beyond its architectural splendor, the Red Fort holds great historical significance. It was from here that the first Prime Minister of India, Jawaharlal Nehru, delivered his iconic Independence Day speech in 1947. Today, the fort attracts millions of visitors each year and stands as a proud symbol of India’s cultural and historical legacy.
